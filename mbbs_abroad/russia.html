Frequently Asked Questions - MBBS in Russia

Get answers to common questions about studying medicine in Russia.

Yes, Russia is generally safe for international students. Major cities like Moscow and St. Petersburg have good infrastructure and Indian communities. Universities provide secure campus environments and many have dedicated international student support services.
FMGE pass rates for Russian universities range from 20-25%. Moscow State University has the highest pass rate around 24%, followed by St. Petersburg State Medical University at 22%. The pass rates are competitive and students receive comprehensive preparation.
Yes, many Russian universities offer MBBS programs in English medium for international students. However, some universities may offer bilingual programs (English + Russian). We recommend universities with complete English-medium instruction that are NMC-approved.
Monthly living expenses in Russia range from ₹20,000-31,000 including food, transportation, and miscellaneous expenses. Hostel fees are ₹1-1.8 lakhs per year. Food costs are higher than in some other destinations, but the quality of education justifies the investment.
Yes, you can practice medicine in India after completing MBBS from Russia. All recommended universities are NMC-approved, so you are eligible to appear for FMGE/NExT. After clearing the screening test, you can register with State Medical Council and practice in India.
Russian universities provide excellent clinical training with access to large hospitals and diverse patient populations. The internship year includes rotations in various departments including medicine, surgery, pediatrics, obstetrics, and gynecology. Students gain extensive hands-on experience under expert supervision.
