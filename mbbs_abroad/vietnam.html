Frequently Asked Questions - MBBS in Vietnam

Get answers to common questions about studying medicine in Vietnam.

Vietnam offers affordable MBBS education with good FMGE pass rates and modern medical facilities. Hanoi Medical University provides quality education at competitive prices with English-medium instruction and growing Indian student community.
FMGE pass rates for Vietnamese universities range from 15-20%. Hanoi Medical University has the highest pass rate around 18%, followed by University of Medicine and Pharmacy, HCMC at 17%. The pass rates are competitive with good preparation.
Yes, Vietnam is generally safe for international students. Cities like Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City have good infrastructure and public transport. Universities provide secure campus environments and there is a growing Indian student community.
Monthly living expenses in Vietnam range from ₹15,000-24,000 including food, transportation, and miscellaneous expenses. Hostel fees are ₹0.6-0.8 lakhs per year. The cost of living is reasonable and affordable for international students.
Yes, you can practice medicine in India after completing MBBS from Vietnam. All recommended universities are NMC-approved, so you are eligible to appear for FMGE/NExT. After clearing the screening test, you can register with State Medical Council and practice in India.
MBBS in Vietnam is a 6-year program including 1 year of internship. The curriculum follows international standards with emphasis on practical training. First 5 years focus on basic sciences and clinical subjects, final year is internship. Teaching is in English with modern facilities.
