Frequently Asked Questions - MBBS in Iraq

Get answers to common questions about studying medicine in Iraq.

Iraq offers affordable MBBS education with good FMGE pass rates and modern medical facilities. University of Baghdad provides quality education at competitive prices with English-medium instruction and rich cultural heritage.
FMGE pass rates for Iraqi universities range from 20-25%. University of Baghdad has the highest pass rate around 25%, followed by University of Basrah at 22%. The pass rates are competitive with good preparation.
Yes, Iraq is safe for international students. Baghdad and other major cities have good infrastructure and public transport. Universities provide secure campus environments and there is a growing Indian student community. We provide safety guidelines and support.
Monthly living expenses in Iraq range from ₹11,000-19,000 including food, transportation, and miscellaneous expenses. Hostel fees are ₹0.4-0.5 lakhs per year. The cost of living is very affordable for international students.
Yes, you can practice medicine in India after completing MBBS from Iraq. All recommended universities are NMC-approved, so you are eligible to appear for FMGE/NExT. After clearing the screening test, you can register with State Medical Council and practice in India.
MBBS in Iraq is a 6-year program including 1 year of internship. The curriculum follows international standards with emphasis on practical training. First 5 years focus on basic sciences and clinical subjects, final year is internship. Teaching is in English with modern facilities.
