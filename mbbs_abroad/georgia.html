Frequently Asked Questions - MBBS in Georgia

Get answers to common questions about studying medicine in Georgia.

Yes, Georgia is one of the safest countries in Europe for international students. It has a low crime rate, stable political environment, and a welcoming attitude towards Indian students. Tbilisi, the capital, has a large Indian community and many Indian restaurants.
The FMGE pass rate for Georgia universities ranges from 25-30%. Tbilisi State Medical University has the highest pass rate at around 28%, followed by European University at 26%. The pass rates are higher than many other destinations and students receive excellent clinical training.
No entrance exam is required for admission to MBBS in Georgia. Admission is based on NEET qualification and 12th standard marks. Students need to have qualified NEET and completed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology with minimum 50% marks (40% for reserved categories).
Monthly living expenses in Georgia range from ₹16,000-25,000 including food, transportation, and miscellaneous expenses. Hostel fees are ₹80,000-1.5 lakhs per year. Food is affordable with Indian mess facilities available in most universities. Public transportation is cheap and safe.
Yes, you can practice medicine in India after completing MBBS from Georgia. All Georgian universities are NMC-approved, so you are eligible to appear for FMGE/NExT. After clearing the screening test, you can register with State Medical Council and practice in India.
MBBS in Georgia is a 6-year program including 1 year of internship. The curriculum follows European standards with emphasis on practical training. First 3 years focus on basic sciences, next 3 years on clinical subjects. Teaching is in English with international faculty. The course is designed to prepare students for global medical practice.
