Frequently Asked Questions - MBBS in Italy

Get answers to common questions about studying medicine in Italy.

Italy offers exceptional medical education with high FMGE pass rates (85-90%), world-class research facilities, and European recognition. Universities like University of Milan provide excellent clinical training and international exposure.
Italian universities have excellent FMGE pass rates ranging from 85-90%. University of Milan has the highest pass rate around 88%, followed by University of Bologna at 86%. The comprehensive preparation ensures high success rates.
Yes, Italy is very safe for international students. Cities like Milan, Bologna, and Rome have excellent infrastructure, public transport, and a welcoming environment. Universities provide secure campus environments and support services.
Monthly living expenses in Italy range from ₹28,000-40,000 including food, transportation, and miscellaneous expenses. Hostel fees are ₹2.0-2.5 lakhs per year. The cost of living is higher than other European destinations but offers excellent value.
Yes, you can practice medicine in India after completing MBBS from Italy. All recommended universities are NMC-approved, so you are eligible to appear for FMGE/NExT. After clearing the screening test, you can register with State Medical Council and practice in India.
MBBS in Italy is a 6-year program including 1 year of internship. The curriculum follows European standards with emphasis on practical training and research. First 5 years focus on basic sciences and clinical subjects, final year is internship. Teaching is in English with international faculty.
