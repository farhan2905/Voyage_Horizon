Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about study visa services.

Common documents include: valid passport, admission letter from the university, academic transcripts and certificates, English proficiency test scores (IELTS/TOEFL), financial documents showing sufficient funds, statement of purpose, letters of recommendation, health insurance, and passport-size photographs. Additional documents may be required depending on the destination country.
Financial requirements vary by country. USA typically requires showing funds for one year of tuition plus living expenses (approximately $25,000-$50,000). UK requires showing tuition fees plus £1,334/month (London) or £1,023/month (outside London) for up to 9 months. Canada requires proof of $20,635 plus first-year tuition. Australia requires showing AUD$24,505 plus tuition. We help you understand specific requirements for your destination.
Yes, most countries allow international students to work part-time. USA: 20 hours/week on-campus during semester, full-time during breaks. UK: 20 hours/week during term time. Canada: 20 hours/week off-campus. Australia: 48 hours/fortnight during study period. Germany: 120 full days or 240 half days per year. Work rights help students gain experience and support living expenses.
Our study visa success rate is 98%, which is among the highest in the industry. This high rate is achieved through thorough profile assessment, complete and accurate documentation, proper financial planning, and comprehensive interview preparation. We identify potential issues early and address them before submission, significantly improving approval chances.
The complete process typically takes 4-8 months, including: university selection and application (1-2 months), receiving offer letter (1-2 months), visa documentation (2-4 weeks), visa processing (2-8 weeks depending on country), and pre-departure preparation (2-4 weeks). We recommend starting 6-12 months before your intended intake to ensure ample time for each stage.
