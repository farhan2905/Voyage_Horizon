Eligibility Criteria

Basic requirements for MBBS admission abroad.

Academic Requirements

  • 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum 50% marks (40% for reserved categories)
  • English as a compulsory subject
  • Valid NEET scorecard (mandatory)

Age & Documents

  • Minimum age: 17 years by December 31
  • Valid passport (minimum 18 months validity)
  • Medical fitness certificate
  • HIV/AIDS test report

Admission Process

Simple steps to secure your MBBS admission abroad.

1

Profile Evaluation

Submit your academic documents and NEET scorecard for a free profile assessment. Our counselors evaluate your eligibility and recommend suitable universities based on your preferences and budget.

2

University Selection

Choose from NMC-approved universities based on factors like fees, FMGE pass rate, infrastructure, and Indian student community. We provide detailed comparisons to help you make an informed decision.

3

Document Submission

Submit required documents including 10th & 12th certificates, NEET scorecard, passport, photographs, and medical certificates. We verify all documents and prepare your application file.

4

Admission Confirmation

Receive your admission letter within 7-15 working days. We assist with apostille and legalization of documents as required by the destination country.

5

Visa & Departure

Complete visa formalities with our assistance. We arrange airport pickup, accommodation, and provide comprehensive pre-departure orientation to ensure a smooth transition.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about MBBS abroad.

Yes, NEET qualification is mandatory for Indian students pursuing MBBS abroad as per NMC (formerly MCI) guidelines. Students must have a valid NEET scorecard to be eligible for admission to foreign medical universities. The NEET score is valid for 3 years for MBBS abroad admissions. Without NEET qualification, students cannot practice in India after completing their degree.
Yes, you can practice in India after completing MBBS from an NMC-approved foreign university. You need to clear the FMGE (Foreign Medical Graduate Examination) or the upcoming NExT (National Exit Test) to obtain a license to practice in India. Our FMGE coaching support helps students prepare for this screening test during their final years of MBBS.
The total cost varies by country and includes tuition fees, hostel fees, food, and other living expenses. Georgia: ₹25-35 Lakhs total for 6 years. Russia: ₹20-35 Lakhs total. Kyrgyzstan: ₹15-25 Lakhs total. Kazakhstan: ₹20-30 Lakhs total. This is significantly lower than Indian private medical colleges which cost ₹50-100 Lakhs for the complete course.
Yes, most universities we recommend offer MBBS programs in English medium. Universities in Georgia, Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, and Philippines have complete English-medium courses. Some Russian universities offer bilingual programs (English + Russian). We only recommend universities with proper English-medium instruction that are approved by NMC.
Most universities offer on-campus hostels with separate accommodation for boys and girls. Indian mess facilities are available in many universities, especially in Georgia and Kyrgyzstan. Monthly food expenses range from ₹5,000-15,000 depending on the country and your lifestyle. We assist with hostel booking and provide details about Indian food options near each university.
